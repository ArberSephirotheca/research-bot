- [Performance of the fusion](https://cug.org/proceedings/cug2014_proceedings/includes/files/pap131-file2.pdf) — 2026-04-15
  - Authors: n/a
  - Affiliations: not provided
  - Fusion can significantly reduce GPU runtime by cutting global memory traffic and kernel-launch overhead; the biggest wins occur when intermediate tensors would otherwise be written to and reread from DRAM.
  - For matrix multiplication–centric workloads, fusion helps most when surrounding elementwise ops (bias, activation, scaling, reductions) can be fused into the GEMM epilogue/prologue; standalone GEMM is typically compute-bound, so fusing unrelated stages yields limited benefit unless it improves data reuse.
  - Benchmark results indicate fusion benefits are workload- and shape-dependent: gains shrink when kernels are already near peak occupancy/throughput or when fusion increases register/shared-memory pressure enough to reduce occupancy.
